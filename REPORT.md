### 📋 Strategic Recommendations

1. **Maximize Tourism Infrastructure in Top Hubs:**  
   Cities like **New York/NJ**, **Los Angeles**, and **Dallas** account for a disproportionate share of potential revenue. Investments in rapid transit between airports and stadiums are critical here.

2. **Capitalize on the Expanded Format (48 Teams):**  
   With 104 matches, the total volume of visitors will be unprecedented. Local organizing committees must prepare for longer overall stays compared to previous 32-team tournaments.

3. **Cross-Border Economic Corridors:**  
   Given the joint hosting with Canada and Mexico, cities like Seattle and Houston should optimize temporary cross-border economic packages to capture transiting fan spending.

4. **Post-Event Legacy:**  
   Unlike previous host nations that spent heavily on new stadiums, the USA already possesses the infrastructure. The projected $4.7B revenue represents direct economic injection with minimal sunk costs.
